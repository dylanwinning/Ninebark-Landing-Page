Pilot Program

Become a Pilot Partner

Help shape the future of classroom calculators. We're looking for a small number of educators to pilot Ninebark and provide feedback from real classroom use.

What you'll receive

  • Free access to Ninebark during the pilot
  • Direct support from the founder
  • Opportunity to influence future features

What we're asking

  • Students use Ninebark occasionally throughout the Fall 2026 semester
  • Share occasional feedback about what worked and what didn't
  • Participate in a brief follow-up conversation

Who we're looking for

Middle and high school educators interested in trying new instructional technology.

These pilot programs will help us refine Ninebark with feedback from educators before a broader launch.

FAQs

Frequently Asked Questions

Who is building Ninebark?

Dylan is a student at Haverford College studying Mathematics and Philosophy.

Is Ninebark AI?

No. Ninebark is a deterministic calculator that interprets natural language with fixed rules, so it always gives the same result for the same input.

Where is Ninebark in its development?

Ninebark's prototype has been built and is suitable for use in pilots.

What math can Ninebark currently support?

Ninebark supports almost everything a TI-84 can. A complete list of its mathematical capabilities is available in the prototype.
